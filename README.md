# Cyber Resilience in Firms, Organizations, and Societies — Student Research Paper

## Overview
This research paper examines cyber resilience as a multi-level concept involving organizations, individuals, threat actors, governments, and insurers. The study reviews existing literature to identify the core components of cyber resilience, distinguishes between threat and non-threat actors, and explores how resources, competence, technology, and strategic decision-making contribute to an organization's ability to withstand and recover from cyber incidents.

## Key Topics Covered
- Definition and core characteristics of cyber resilience
- Differences between cybersecurity and cyber resilience
- Factors influencing cyber resilience (resources, competence, technology, tools, strategy)
- The relationship between cyber resilience and cyber insurance
- Cyber resilience across different levels (nations, organizations, IT systems)
- Future challenges introduced by IoT and AI
- Real-world applications: critical infrastructure and military systems
- Measurement challenges and proposed frameworks for evaluating resilience
